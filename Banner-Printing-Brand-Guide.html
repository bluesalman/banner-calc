01 — Identity

Logo

The Banner Printing logo features a transparent background with "BANNER" set in bold black and supplementary text in printing industry colors (cyan, magenta, and accent tones). Two variants exist: the standard logo for light backgrounds and the white logo for dark backgrounds.

Primary — White BG

Clear Space

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the cap-height of the "B" in BANNER on all sides. No text, icons, or graphic elements should intrude into this zone.

Minimum Size

Digital: 140px wide minimum for full lockup. Print: 35mm wide minimum. The multi-color elements lose legibility below these thresholds.

✓ Do

Use provided logo files only. Place standard logo on white or light backgrounds. Use the white variant (banner-printing-logo-w.png) on dark backgrounds. Maintain original color relationships. Pair with the CMYK accent bar for added brand recognition. Keep proportions locked.

✗ Don't

Never recreate the logo in a typeface. Never change the BANNER text color from black (standard) or white (dark variant). Never place the standard logo on dark backgrounds — use the white variant instead. Never stretch, rotate, or add effects (drop shadow, glow, bevel).

04 — Brand Voice

Tone & Messaging

Banner Printing speaks like the expert maker next door — knowledgeable, efficient, and genuinely helpful. We're a UK-based production house, not a faceless factory. Every message should feel fast, friendly, and straight-up reliable.

🏭

Maker's Pride

We produce everything in-house. That's a huge differentiator — lean into it. "Printed, packed, and shipped from our UK facility." Craftsmanship language builds trust.

⚡

Fast & No-Fuss

Speed is core to the brand. "24–48 hour dispatch." "Next-day delivery." Keep sentences punchy. Active voice. Short paragraphs. Eliminate waffle — customers are busy.

💬

Friendly Expert

Not corporate stiff, not overly casual. Think helpful shopkeeper who really knows print. Use "we" and "you." Offer guidance without jargon: "hemmed edges" not "heat-welded perimeter reinforcement."

💷

Transparent on Price

"No hidden fees" is a brand promise. Always be upfront about costs, delivery, and what's included. Free shipping messaging should be prominent but not shouty.

🌱

Eco-Conscious

Sustainability is genuine, not greenwashing. Mention recycled materials, PVC-free options, and greener packaging when relevant. Factual, not preachy.

🇬🇧

Proudly British

"Made in the UK" carries weight. Use British English consistently (colour, customise, centre). Reference UK locations and delivery coverage. Appeal to local trust while serving globally.

06 — Procedure

How to Create Any Asset

Follow these steps for every piece of content — social posts, website sections, email templates, packaging inserts, signage, and print materials — to ensure brand consistency everywhere.

Choose Your Canvas

Select the correct dimensions for the platform:

  • Instagram / Facebook Feed: 1080×1080 (1:1) or 1080×1350 (4:5)
  • Instagram / FB Story: 1080×1920 (9:16)
  • Facebook Cover: 820×312
  • Website Hero: 1920×700
  • Email Header: 600×200
  • Product Card: 800×800 (1:1)
  • Packaging Insert: A5 landscape (210×148mm)

Set Background

Pick one of the approved backgrounds:

  • White (#FFFFFF) — Default for most content and product displays
  • Off-White (#F8F8F9) — Alternating sections, softer feel
  • Key Black (#1A1A1A) — Hero sections, promotions, dark templates
  • Yellow (#FFF200) — Flash sales and urgent promotions only
  • Cyan (#00AEEF) — CTA-focused, delivery/dispatch highlights

Place the CMYK Bar

The 4-colour CMYK stripe is a mandatory brand element:

  • Always Cyan → Magenta → Yellow → Black, equal widths
  • Position: top or bottom edge of every asset
  • Height: 4–6px on social, 8–12px on large formats
  • Full width of the canvas — never truncated
  • This is the brand's most recognisable visual marker

Place the Logo

Use the correct logo variant for your background:

  • White/light backgrounds: Standard logo (banner-printing-logo-554150.png)
  • Dark backgrounds: White logo variant (banner-printing-logo-w.png)
  • Position: top-left for layouts, top-center for stories, bottom-right for cards
  • Minimum 140px wide (digital) / 35mm (print)

Apply Typography

Follow the type hierarchy strictly:

  • Overline: IBM Plex Mono 500, 11px, uppercase, 3px tracking, Cyan colour
  • Headline: Outfit 800–900, 22–56px depending on format
  • Body: Plus Jakarta Sans 400, 14–16px, secondary text colour
  • CTA Button: Outfit 700, 11–13px, uppercase, 1px tracking
  • Product Spec: IBM Plex Mono 400, 13–14px

Final Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

  • ☐ CMYK bar present (Cyan → Magenta → Yellow → Black)
  • ☐ Logo is legible on chosen background
  • ☐ Colours are exact hex values — not approximations
  • ☐ Outfit for headlines, Plus Jakarta Sans for body
  • ☐ Cyan (#00AEEF) is the leading accent colour
  • ☐ Sufficient whitespace — don't overcrowd
  • ☐ British English spelling throughout
  • ☐ Copy tone is friendly, fast, and expert
  • ☐ Exported at correct dimensions for platform

01 — Essence

Fast. Vibrant. UK-Made.

Banner Printing UK is a Manchester-based custom print company producing high-impact vinyl banners, mesh banners, roller standees, backdrops, and exhibition displays — delivered across the UK within 24 to 48 hours. Every job is produced in-house at our Denton facility: no outsourcing, no delays, no compromises.

02 — Our Story

The Origin

Banner Printing UK was founded on a single conviction: every business — from a first-time market trader to a national event company — deserves professional, high-impact print without inflated costs or unreliable timelines.

Operating from our production facility in Denton, Manchester, we print, pack, and dispatch everything in-house. No overseas supply chains. No middlemen. Just clean, vibrant, weather-ready print — delivered across the UK, usually within 24 to 48 hours of ordering.

We have fulfilled over 2,000 custom orders and carry a 4.9-star rating — not because it is a goal, but because it is what naturally follows when you take lead times seriously, answer questions honestly, and care about the quality of what you ship.

Whether it is a single roll-up standee for a pop-up shop or a 10-metre backdrop for a trade show, the standard stays the same: premium material, sharp colour, on time.

11 — Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Use clean, high-resolution product photography on neutral or solid-colour backgrounds
  • Keep layouts bold and uncluttered — let print quality and colour do the talking
  • Be specific with every claim: gsm, DPI, lead time, price
  • Use the Ink Black / Hot Pink / Electric Yellow palette consistently
  • Write concise, confident copy — every word should earn its place
  • Show real customer installs at markets, festivals, storefronts
  • Confirm artwork specs with the customer before going to print on large orders
  • Use the full logo mark (icon + wordmark) together at all times

✗ Don't

  • Use excessive emojis, all-caps hype language, or "SALE SALE SALE" energy
  • Clutter designs with competing typefaces, gradients, or off-brand colours
  • Use RGB colour mode in artwork files — always convert to CMYK
  • Separate the icon from the wordmark — they must always appear together
  • Use vague claims like "affordable" or "great quality" — back every claim with a number
  • Recreate the logo in type or modify its proportions
  • Post content featuring competitor branding or clip art
  • Ignore bleed and safe zone requirements
